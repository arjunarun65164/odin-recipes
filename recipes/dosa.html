Dosa Recipe

Description

In a large bowl, combine 2 cups of regular rice and 1 cup of urad dal (split black lentils) with ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds. Rinse them well and soak in water separately for 4-6 hours. After soaking, drain the water from the rice and dal. Grind them separately to a smooth consistency using a blender or a wet grinder, adding water gradually as needed. Mix the ground rice and dal together in a bowl, add salt to taste, and whisk the batter thoroughly. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter. Allow the batter to ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours, covering the bowl with a lid.

Heat a non-stick or cast-iron griddle (tawa) over medium heat. Once hot, sprinkle a few drops of water on the tawa – if it sizzles, the tawa is ready. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the tawa and quickly spread it outward in a circular motion to form a thin, round dosa. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and a few drops on the top. Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy, then flip it over using a spatula. Cook the other side for a minute or so until it turns light golden. Remove the dosa from the tawa and serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, or any desired accompaniments.
